## FAQs

**How does Bath's Kennebec River location affect roofing?**
The Kennebec River brings salt spray miles inland and creates constant high humidity. This accelerates corrosion and demands superior moisture management. We use marine-grade fasteners, enhanced ventilation, and moisture barriers specifically designed for Bath's maritime climate.

**Do you work on historic Victorian homes in Bath?**
Yes. We specialize in Bath's historic shipbuilding district Victorian architecture. We match materials to original designs, preserve architectural details, and work within historic district requirements while upgrading performance.

**What roofing materials last longest in Bath's climate?**
Standing seam metal with Kynar coatings offers the longest lifespan (50+ years) and best salt spray resistance. For historic homes, architectural shingles with enhanced warranties perform well when installed with proper ventilation and moisture barriers.

**Do you handle permits in Bath?**
Yes. We manage all permitting with Bath's Building Department and can assist with historic district design review when required. We're familiar with local preservation standards and maritime climate building codes.

**Can you install metal roofing on Victorian homes?**
It depends on the property and historic district requirements. Some Victorian homes accept standing seam metal beautifully, while others require period-correct asphalt or slate. We'll review architectural compatibility during your assessment.
