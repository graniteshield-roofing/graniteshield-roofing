## FAQs

**How do Turner's snow loads affect roofing requirements?**
Turner receives significantly heavier snow accumulation than coastal areas—50+ PSF vs 30 PSF at the coast. We engineer roof framing, rafter sizing, and material selection specifically for these loads. Standard coastal systems can fail structurally under Turner's snow.

**Can you handle rural properties with long driveways?**
Yes. We're equipped for rural accessibility challenges—self-sufficient crews, proper equipment, and efficient logistics. We coordinate material delivery and staging for remote locations.

**Do you work on barns and agricultural buildings?**
Absolutely. Agricultural buildings require specialized roofing—larger spans, ventilation for livestock/equipment, and durability for working environments. We're experienced with barn roofing, outbuilding restoration, and farm structure needs.

**What roofing material works best for Turner's climate?**
Standing seam metal offers superior snow shedding and 50+ year lifespan—ideal for both homes and agricultural buildings. CertainTeed Integrity asphalt systems also perform well with proper ventilation and ice dam prevention. We'll recommend based on your building type and budget.

**How do you prevent ice dams in Turner?**
Proper ventilation (ridge and soffit) combined with insulation coordination prevents the temperature differential that causes ice dams. We also install ice & water shield at eaves and valleys for backup protection when ice does form.
